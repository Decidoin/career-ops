# Mode: Negotiate

Triggered by: `/career-ops negotiate`

## Workflow

1. Ask: Which company/role? What's the offer?
2. Read the evaluation report
3. Analyze the offer against:
   - $300K floor
   - Market comp data from the evaluation
   - Equity structure and liquidity timeline
   - Benefits, signing bonus, relocation
4. Generate negotiation strategy

## Frameworks

**Below floor ($300K):**
- Calculate the gap
- Identify leverage: competing offers, unique skills, visa sponsorship value
- Draft counter: "I'm excited about the role. My target is $X based on [market data, scope, my track record]. Can we bridge the gap with [base, equity, signing bonus]?"
- Prepare walk-away line: "I appreciate the offer, but at this level I'd need to decline."

**At floor:**
- Push for equity upside, signing bonus, or accelerated review
- Negotiate scope/title if comp is fixed

**Above floor:**
- Focus on equity, scope, reporting line, publishing rights, conference budget
- Negotiate for O-1A-friendly terms: "critical role" language in offer letter, research time allocation

## Geographic Discount Pushback

If they cite Dubai/remote as a reason for lower comp:
"The role is global in scope. I'm competing with candidates in SF and NYC. My output isn't discounted by timezone. If the role were location-limited, I'd understand — but it's not."

## Visa Negotiation

- Request visa sponsorship early, not as an afterthought
- Frame O-1A as low-risk for employer: "My petition is already in process. The employer's role is limited — sign the support letter."
- Negotiate for "critical role" framing in the employment agreement (strengthens O-1A evidence)
